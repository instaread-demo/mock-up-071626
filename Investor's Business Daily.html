Dow Jones futures fell modestly Tuesday night, along with S&P 500 futures and Nasdaq futures, with several notable earnings and news after the close. Dow Jones futures lost 0.9%, with losses concentrated in chip and AI plays.

South Korea’s Kospi index fell sharply overnight as SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics tumbled. The Bank of Korea raised its key rate by a quarter point, as expected.

The 10-year Treasury yield rose slightly to 4.57%.

Remember that overnight action in Dow futures and elsewhere doesn’t necessarily translate into actual trading in the next regular stock market session.

Key Earnings & News

Apple (AAPL) stock fell 2% in extended trading after hitting an all-time high during the regular session. Alphabet (GOOGL) dipped 1.5% on reports of antitrust concerns.

Dell Technologies (DELL) and Western Digital/SanDisk also retreated in after-hours action, sliding 3%-4%.

Micron Technology (MU) extended its regular-session selloff, falling another 2% after hours. The memory-chip giant tumbled 8% during the day on concerns about AI demand sustainability.

SpaceX reportedly priced its latest tender offer at $350 per share, valuing Elon Musk’s space venture at roughly $350 billion.

Taiwan Semiconductor (TSM) will report earnings Thursday before the open. Analysts expect strong results driven by AI chip demand.

GE Aerospace (GE) also reports Thursday. The jet-engine maker is expected to show continued strength in commercial aviation services.

What To Do Now

The stock market rally has shown resilience, but leadership is narrowing. The rotation out of megacap tech and into other areas is a healthy sign, but investors should be selective.

Focus on stocks breaking out of proper bases with strong fundamentals. Keep your exposure in line with the current market conditions, and don’t chase extended stocks.
